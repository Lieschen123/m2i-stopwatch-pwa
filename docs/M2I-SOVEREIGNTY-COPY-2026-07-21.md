*Created: 2026-07-21*

## Working positioning

Move2Improve is not a fitness tracker.

It is a commitment device for small groups: you choose a challenge, put a little skin in the game, prove completion locally, and share only the receipt you choose to share.

## Core claims

### 1. Proof without surveillance

M2I does not need your raw body data to coordinate a challenge.

The app creates a signed claim on your device. The claim says what you say you completed and when you signed it. It does not upload routes, heart-rate streams, camera footage, or private health history.

### 2. Your body data stays yours

Fitness apps usually turn movement into a cloud account.

M2I treats movement as something you can prove locally. The group only sees what you deliberately share.

### 3. The receipt proves the claim, not the body

M2I V1 is honest about trust.

A signed self-attestation proves who claimed what and when. It does not pretend to be cheat-proof. For friends and small groups, that is a feature: enough structure to make a commitment real, without turning the body into a surveillance object.

### 4. Stakes are social, manual, and non-custodial

M2I does not hold money, pay automatically, or monitor settlement.

If someone misses, the group settles manually: sats, USDt, euros, team jar, or whatever the group chose. The app coordinates the rule. People handle the money.

### 5. Built for small groups first

M2I is for 2-8 people who know each other and want a real commitment.

Not a mass leaderboard. Not a stranger casino. Not a corporate wellness dashboard. A private challenge between people whose word still matters.
